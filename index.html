Billie Eilish

Billie Eilish was born in Los Angeles California,USA, first gained recognition in 2015 for the song "Ocean Eyes" and became, in 2020, the youngest person ever to win a grammy.
Her other works include Happier than ever (2021) and the award-winning song "what was I made for?".

Billie's Life
  • Billie's parents were inspired by Hanson, a 90's pop band formed by a group of homeschooled brothers from Tulsa,Oklahoma
  • As a child billie experimented with horseback riding, dance, and singing as a member of the Los Angeles Children's Chorus.
  • In 2015 Eilish’s elder brother, Finneas Baird O’Connell, who performs under the moniker FINNEAS, enlisted her to sing “Ocean Eyes,” a song he had written and produced for his band at the time and later put online.
  • Eilish’s music draws inspiration from hip-hop artists such as Childish Gambino and Tyler, the Creator.
  • Her debut album, When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go?, was released on March 29, 2019, and reached number one on the Billboard 200 chart.
